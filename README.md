# SuburbIVU: Urban Green Infrastructure in Rio de Janeiro's Suburbs

As urban heat islands and heat waves become increasingly intense, understanding how different types of urban green infrastructure (UGI) contribute to ecosystem services is critical for developing climate adaptation and mitigation strategies in cities. This is especially important to ensure that urban well-being is distributed equitably (Anguelovski, 2018).

Rio de Janeiro has a long history of urban inequality, reflected in distinct patterns of land use planning and occupation across the city. In the suburbs, wooded backyards and vegetated common areas are widespread. Although little studied, these small green spaces may play an important role in delivering ecological and social benefits (Ruffato-Ferreira, 2016).

## Objectives

- (1) Understand the distribution of urban green infrastructure (UGI) across the suburbs and whether it is associated with socioeconomic, demographic, and land tenure history factors;
- (2.1) Evaluate the relative contribution of green private gardens to ecosystem service provision in Rio de Janeiro's suburbs;
- (2.2) Understand how this contribution relates to ladscape structure (i.e. landscape configuration and landscape composition);
- (2.3) Explore the limitations, spatial variation, scales, and magnitude of UGI effects on the suburban landscape;
